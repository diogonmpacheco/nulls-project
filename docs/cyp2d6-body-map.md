# CYP2D6 Body Map

This map supports the systemic-null framing for `CYP2D6`.

The core point:

> A true inherited CYP2D6 null is not just a medication label. It can mean inherited absence of CYP2D6 function wherever CYP2D6 is normally active.

The boundary:

> Not every body compartment has equal evidence, and not every phenotype is known.

## Compartments In v0

### Liver / Systemic Circulation

This is the strongest and most familiar layer. CYP2D6 nulls can reduce activation of some prodrugs and increase parent exposure for other substrates.

### Gut / First-Pass Entry

Gut handling is a source of uncertainty. Absorption, transporters, food exposures, microbiome effects, and first-pass metabolism can all change what reaches plasma and liver.

### Brain / CNS Local Enzyme

This is the key reason CYP2D6 belongs in Nulls rather than only in a pharmacogenomics panel. Human brain expression and functional evidence make the CNS a real compartment to track.

### Blood-Brain Barrier / CNS Entry

Barrier transport and local biotransformation can make CNS exposure diverge from plasma exposure. An inhibitor that phenoconverts liver CYP2D6 may not reproduce inherited CNS enzyme absence.

### Kidney / Urine Biomarkers

Urine can show CYP2D6-derived metabolite patterns. This is often a readout of upstream metabolism, not proof that kidney-local CYP2D6 is the causal compartment.

### Cardiovascular Target Effects

For beta-blockers and antiarrhythmics, the strongest evidence is often systemic exposure reaching cardiovascular targets. That should not be confused with proof of local cardiac CYP2D6 null biology.

### Adipose / Lipid Reservoirs

For lipophilic substrates, distribution and release may matter. This is currently a pharmacokinetic distribution question more than a proven adipose-local CYP2D6 mechanism.

### Endocrine / Reproductive Tissues

This remains open. The atlas should track local expression, substrate evidence, and inhibitor distribution before making phenotype claims.

### Immune / Inflammatory Interface

This is a hypothesis layer for pathway load and exposure. It should not be presented as an established immune phenotype.

### Other Peripheral Tissues

The map leaves room for lung, skin, muscle, connective tissue, spleen, and other compartments. These should stay unresolved until tissue-specific evidence exists.

## Main Rule

For every compartment, ask:

- is CYP2D6 expressed or functional there?
- is the substrate endogenous, external, or both?
- does inherited absence matter locally?
- does medication-induced inhibition reach the same compartment?
- is the observed effect local metabolism or systemic exposure reaching a target?
- what is the evidence tier?

This keeps the body-wide null frame serious without overclaiming.
